Consider the following equation in real \(x\),\(y\): \({(x-2y-1)^2 + (4x-3y-4)^2 + (x-2y-1)(4x-3y-4)=0}\) Then the number of solutions \((x,y)\) is:
Solution:
Clearly, the equation can be rewritten as:
\([(x-2y-1)+(4x-3y-4)]^2-(x-2y-1)(4x-3y-4)=0\)
\(or,[(x-2y-1)+(4x-3y-4)]^2=(x-2y-1)(4x-3y-4)\)
\(or,[5(x-y-1)]^2=(x-2y-1)(4x-3y-4)\)
\(or,25(x-y-1)^2=[(x-y-1)-y][4(x-y-1)+y]\)
\(or,25(x-y-1)^2=4(x-y-1)^2+(x-y-1)y+(x-y-1)4y-y^2\)
\(or,25=4+\dfrac{y}{(x-y-1)}+\dfrac{4y}{(x-y-1)}-\dfrac{y^2}{(x-y-1)^2}\)
\(or,21(x-y-1)^2+3y(x-y-1)+y^2=0\)
Substituting: \([t=(x-y-1)]\);
\(or,21t^2+3yt+y^2=0\)
\(Taking, \Delta\);
\(or,9y^2-84y^2 \ge 0 [since, x,y âˆˆ \Bbb{R}]\)
\(or,y=0\)
\(Therefore, t=(x-y-1)=0\)
\(x=1\) Further, there exists two indistinct real roots of \(x-y-1\), \(1,0\) will be the only solution for \(x,y\) in real.
